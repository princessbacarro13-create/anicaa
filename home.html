What is Science?

Science is the human pursuit of understanding the universe through observation and evidence rather than tradition, intuition, or authority. It is less a "body of knowledge" and more a "way of thinking"‚Äîa rigorous commitment to checking our ideas against reality.

The Philosophy of Systematic Doubt

Science begins with the humble admission that we might be wrong. This is known as skepticism. A scientist doesn't just look for evidence that supports their idea; they actively try to prove themselves wrong. This process, called falsifiability, ensures that only the most robust ideas survive.

This journey is guided by empiricism, the belief that knowledge comes primarily from sensory experience. If a claim cannot be measured, tested, or observed in some way, it falls outside the realm of science and into the realm of philosophy or faith.

The Engine of Discovery: The Scientific Method

The process of science is a repeating loop designed to strip away human bias. It typically starts with an observation that leads to a "why" or "how" question. From there, a scientist forms a hypothesis‚Äîa specific, testable prediction.

The heart of the process is the experiment, where variables are controlled to see if the hypothesis holds up under pressure. If the data contradicts the hypothesis, the idea is discarded or refined. If the data supports it, the results are shared with the global community for peer review.

The Tree of Knowledge: Main Disciplines

Physical Sciences focus on the laws of the non-living universe, including Physics, Chemistry, and Astronomy.

Life Sciences focus on living organisms, from Genetics and Microbiology to Ecology and Zoology.

Earth and Space Sciences examine our planet and its systems, including Geology, Meteorology, and Oceanography.

Social Sciences apply scientific methods to human behavior and societies, such as Psychology and Economics.

Theory vs. Law: A Common Misconception

A Scientific Theory is an exhaustive explanation supported by extensive evidence, while a Scientific Law describes what happens without explaining why.

The Big Picture: Science is a self-correcting map. It doesn‚Äôt provide absolute truth, but it provides the most accurate map of reality we have.
